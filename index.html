Mombasa

Mombasa is Kenya's oldest and second-largest city, located on the country's southeastern coast along the Indian Ocean. It is known for its beautiful beaches, rich Swahili culture, warm tropical climate, and vibrant history shaped by African, Arab, Asian, and European influences. One of its most famous landmarks is Fort Jesus, a UNESCO World Heritage Site built by the Portuguese in 1593. The fort stands as a symbol of Mombasa's historical importance as a major trading port and offers visitors a fascinating glimpse into the city's past through its architecture, artifacts, and panoramic views of the harbor.
